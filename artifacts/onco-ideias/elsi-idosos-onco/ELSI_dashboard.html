Methodology note: All estimates use calibrated survey weights accounting for complex sampling design (PSU, strata). These are independent cross-sectional samples -- changes reflect population-level trends, not individual trajectories. Conditions are based on self-reported doctor diagnosis. Sentinel codes (8/88/888 = not applicable, 9/99/999 = missing) were excluded before estimation.

About the data: ELSI-Brazil (Estudo Longitudinal da Saude dos Idosos Brasileiros) is a nationally representative household survey of adults aged 50+ in Brazil. Complex survey design uses primary sampling units (PSUs) stratified by municipality characteristics. Calibrated weights adjust for non-response and population benchmarks. Ethics: CAAE 34649814.3.0000.5091. Cite: Lima-Costa et al., Am J Epidemiol 2018; Lima-Costa et al., Int J Epidemiol 2023.

Data access: Cross-sectional public use files from elsi.cpqrr.fiocruz.br. Longitudinal linkage requires direct request to elsi@fiocruz.br.
