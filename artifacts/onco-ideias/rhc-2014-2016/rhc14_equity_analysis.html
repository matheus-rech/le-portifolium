Methods: Late-stage defined as clinical Stage III or IV. Odds ratios from multivariable logistic regression adjusting for age (continuous, standardized), sex, cancer site (10 categories), race, education, and region simultaneously. 95% confidence intervals from Wald approximation. Chi-square tests: Race chi2=187.2 (p < 0.001), Education chi2=1013.0 (p < 0.001), Region chi2=2342.7 (p < 0.001). Cramer's V: Race=0.032, Education=0.064, Region=0.087. All tests highly significant but effect sizes are small to moderate, consistent with known social determinants of health literature. Analysis restricted to White, Black, and Pardo racial groups (96.7% of classified records).

Limitations: Hospital-based registry (not population-based), so referral patterns may introduce selection bias. High "unknown" coding for race (30.9%) and education (23.8%) among unstaged records. Staging completeness varies by institution. Cross-sectional design -- cannot establish causality.
